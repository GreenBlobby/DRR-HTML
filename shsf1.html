Science Lab

Overloaded Power Strip

The power strip has three plugs all connected to it. If each plugged-in device uses a lot of electricity, the strip can get too hot. This heat can damage the wires inside or even start a fire.

Heavy Items on Top of Shelves

Large glass globes on the cabinet tops are heavy and not secured in place. If they get knocked over – maybe by someone bumping the cabinet, a fan moving, or even a small shake – they could fall down. This might hurt anyone nearby or break into sharp pieces that can cut people.

Fire and Elctrical Hazards

There’s a lot of paper and cardboard, which burn easily. An electronic device is also on the shelf with messy wires. If the wires get damaged or the device overheats, it could create a spark that sets the paper on fire. The clutter would make the fire spread fast.
