Classroom 1

Stains on Ceiling

The dark stains and worn spots mean water might have gotten in over time. This can make the building materials weak, so more parts could break or fall apart easily.

Sharp and Exposed Point

The nail is sticking out from the green wall with its sharp tip up. If students walk by and bump into it, or reach near the wall without looking, the nail could cut their skin, poke their hands, or scratch their arms and faces.

Poor Socket Maintenance

The wall around the outlet is dirty and has marks, which shows it hasn’t been checked or fixed in a while. If small problems like this are not taken care of, they can turn into big accidents quickly.

Rusty and Sharp Doorframe

The green paint on the door frame is chipped off, and there’s a lot of rust underneath. The rough, sharp edges of the rusty metal can cut students’ hands or arms if they brush against it while walking through the doorway.

Slippery Floor

The shiny, waxed floor makes it easy to slip and fall, especially when walking quickly.
